Teaching and Leadership

I'm passionate about leadership and trying to make an impact on my campus and the people around me. To that end, I served as Diversity Director on the Executive Board of the Associated Students of Harvey Mudd College (ASHMC), HMC's student government. I oversaw and helped diversity-related groups and organizations in their efforts to create a more inclusive atmosphere on campus, and helped support on-campus activities and services designed to promote diversity. Two examples of projects I've helped work on include the Riggs Room, a space for diversity groups on Harvey Mudd's campus, and the ASHMC Textbook Library, a service for students to freely borrow and use textbooks for their core and major classes without having to deal with the financial burden of purchasing them.

In Fall 2018, I served on a Hiring Comimttee to interview and select candidates for Harvey Mudd's Associate Dean of Institutional Diversity. In Fall 2019, I served on a Student Hiring Committee to aid in the selection process for new Computer Science Professors.

During my freshman and sophomore year, I served as Co-President of the Class of 2020, where I put on events to promote interaction and bonding both among students and between students and professors and served as a liaison between students and ASHMC. As a member of Senate, I helped craft policy and voted on various proposals from funding/budgeting requests to changes in ASHMC's constituation.

My sophomore year, I was a Mentor for APISPAM, Harvey Mudd's Asian / Pacific Islander affinity group. I helped mentor API-identifying freshmen and put on events related to issues faced by the API community.

In Fall 2020, I TAed for CS181P (Machine Learning, Info, and Search). In Spring 2019, I TAed for CS 158 (Machine Learning) and CS 140 (Algorithms) at Harvey Mudd College. For these classes, I held office hours, graded assignments, held exam review sessions, and wrote problems. In Fall 2017, I TAed for the advanced section of CS5 (Introduction to Computer Science) at Harvey Mudd College, and for CS35 (Data Science) in Spring of 2018.
